## User Story

> As a user,
> I want to describe how I make money using real-world categories,
> so the system understands my tax exposure and planning flexibility.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] User selects one or more income types:
  - W-2 employment
  - Self-employed / sole proprietor
  - Business owner (LLC / S-Corp)
  - Contract / 1099
  - Rental income
  - Investment income
- [ ] Each income source includes:
  - Recurring vs variable
  - Ability to reduce, defer, or restructure
- [ ] Language avoids tax jargon where possible

---

## Epic Context

**Epic 7: Tax-Relevant Profile & Income Context Modeling**

### Epic Goal

Enable users to express how they make money, how flexible it is, and how taxes affect their choices, so PlanSmart can deliver explicit, goal-driven tax optimization guidance instead of implicit assumptions.

This epic establishes tax awareness as a first-class planning input, not a derived afterthought.

### Epic Success Criteria

- Users can describe income in human terms, not tax forms
- Tax treatment differences are explicitly modeled
- Early-retirement and tax-optimization intent is user-stated
- Downstream projections reference declared tax goals, not guessed behavior

### Feature Group 1: Income Source & Flexibility Modeling

This story belongs to Feature Group 1, which focuses on modeling income sources and their flexibility for tax planning purposes.
